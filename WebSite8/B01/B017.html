There’s no difference in meaning between “afterward” and “afterwards,” but the two are generally used in separate contexts. In American English, “afterward” is more common, whereas in British English and Canadian English, “afterwards” is.

05/02/2021: C13 Edit, P2
Whilst editing P2 I came across an interesting problem –

“…at it’s center” versus “…at it’s centre”.

After consulting grammerly, I have opted for, “…at it’s center” –

Center and centre have the same meaning. Center is the correct spelling in American English, but British English writers usually prefer centre. Notice that center (and centre) can be a noun, adjective, or a verb. Seeing the two words in real-life examples may help you to visualize how to use them.
