Say We’re Sweethearts Again

Harley Quinn or “Dr. Harleen Frances Quinzel” first appears in the cartoon, Batman The Animated Series and I recently watched an episode. Series 2, episode 10, “Harlequinade”, 1994. There is a scene where Harley sings a jazzy tune that has funny yet dark lyrics.

“I never knew. That our romance had ended. Until you poisoned my food. And I thought it was a lark. When you kicked me in the park. But now I think it was rude…”

The song is called, “Say We’re Sweethearts Again” and when I searched for it, I came across a short black and white clip of Virginia O’Brien singing. It was from a movie called “Meet the People” from the 1940’s and the lyrics were exactly the same. The Batman TAS writers were subtle, intelligent and had great taste; jazz, suits, dresses, gangsters, tommy guns, etc.

I like the Batman of the early-to-mid nineties. Tim Burton’s gothic Batman and Batman TAS’s film-noir Batman. Both are connected by Danny Elfman’s music and both seem to be more focused on style, character and fantasy rather than content, plot and reality.

Both the Joker and Harley Quinn, love having fun. However Harley’s fun seems to stem from her childlike, playful spirit and this contrasts with Joker’s more sinister personality. To a certain extent, her “immaturity” accounts for her moral flexibility and this is why I see her as neither a villain or a hero. She has a sense of humor, a pretty face and a mischievous smile.

At the end of this episode, “Harley’s Holiday”, (S3, E25), Batman helps her and she kisses him. There is an intriguing friendship between the two opposing characters. Harley’s playfulness challenges Batman to lighten-up and Batman’s seriousness challenges Harley to focus. They balance each other out.
