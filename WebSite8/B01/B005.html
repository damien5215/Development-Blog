I love Meet Joe Black

A few months ago MJB was on TV and I decided to watch the whole of it, in high definition. Claire Forlani’s eyes. Brad Pitt’s cool. Anthony Hopkins’s power. The movie is atmospheric, subtle, moving and elegant. It belongs to my favourite category; big budget movies that are too artistic to be mainstream yet somehow get away with it. Another good example of this is Anthony Minghella’s adaptation/interpretation of “The Talented Mr Ripley” (1999).

MJB is about taking risks, vulnerability, serendipity, sincerity and connection. It’s also about understanding that everything is transitory and not taking things for granted. This is content fit for literary fiction and I like to think that this accounts for the fact that it has a running time of 3 hours. At 90 million dollars to make, 30 million over budget, that means that one minute of the film cost 500k and it shows. The cinematography is beautiful.

The first time I saw MJB I didn’t really understand what was going on. It was summer, I was in my late-teens and I remember basking in the comfortable atmosphere of the scenes, not worrying about the plot. When I saw MJB, a few months ago, I paid closer attention to the script and the characters, especially “Susan”. Something clicked this time and everything added up. The ending is powerful and when I need to remind myself of how amazing movies can be, I watch it.
