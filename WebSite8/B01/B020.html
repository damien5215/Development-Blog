PLANNING AND GOAL-SETTING
A few months ago, I realized that I wasn’t managing my time effectively. I was spending a lot time planning things but I didn’t action many of them. Then I started watching motivational videos. There is a lot of content out there about discipline, Shaolin monks, the Navy Seals and endurance athletes. I discovered David Goggins and couldn’t stop laughing. I love how extreme he is.

It inspired me I tried to try some extreme exercises but my body was way too unfit for it so I gave up. What it reminded me of is that, having a long term goal and plan, is something you have to chip away at everyday. I keep a diary so I’m good at documenting things however my planning and goal-setting needed some work.

EXCEL SHEETS
I created an excel sheet. The first sheet (Tasks) is a large list of tasks and if they are related, they are stacked next to each other. The second sheet (9 to 5) shows the schedule for the day from 9am to 1pm and 1pm to 5pm. Underneath this is a list of tasks. During the day I decide what to do and when. The S means that the task has been started and the F means it has been finished. Once a task is completed and I don’t need to see it anymore, I move it down with all the other completed tasks.


TWO BIRDS, ONE STONE
Another thing that I try to do, to make tasks more engaging, is to connect them to another more pleasurable activity. When I cook, I also listen to jazz FM hoping for a good tune to come on so I can sing along. Also I used to watch a video (TV) for 20 about minutes whilst holding an abdominal crunch. I need to do that more. Two birds with one stone.

With regard to coding practice, it has become habitual because of my GitHub activity bar. It’s become like a game. Every weekday I try to do at least 4 updates (push code to server). This ensures the green square for the day is a nice shade of green. Actually these green squares are probably what inspired me sub-consciously to make the “completed” squares in the excel sheet green.

NEED AND WANT
Essentially I try to divide all the tasks into two groups. Tasks I “need” to do and tasks I “want” to do. A few months ago when I was bored, I didn’t even know what I wanted to do. There were too many things. Once I wrote everything that I wanted to do on a piece of paper, I was able to organize them and make “actionable” plans. Then I made a list of the tasks that I needed to do (work) and said to myself if I complete task A (need) then I can do task B (want). This gave me incentive and passion to get the “need” tasks done. Being highly aware of what you want, makes you more engaged with the world. I do not live to work, I work to live.

10 MINUTE RULE
Whenever I try to do some work that I’m not interested in, I make myself look at it for 5 to 10 minutes. All I do is read it and then do something else. This gives my sub-conscious time to think about it and eventually I get an idea. Then I tend to dive into the task. This really helps against procrastination and fear. I think it would be a good strategy for the kids in school who start their homework late.

SELF LEARNING
1. Look/watch the material (code). Do not make notes just take it in.
2. Make a list of the main topics that it covered.
3. Go over, one of them, in detail.
4. Do an example (implement something) and listen to music.
5. Move on to next topic.
6. Once completed document what you learned.
