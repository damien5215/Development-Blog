Lockdown Productivity

A few months ago, I realized that I wasn’t managing my time effectively. I was spending a lot time planning things but I didn’t action many of them. Then I started watching motivational videos. There is a lot of content out there about discipline, Shaolin monks, the Navy Seals and endurance athletes. I discovered David Goggins and couldn’t stop laughing. I love how extreme he is.

It inspired me I tried to try some extreme exercises but my body was way too unfit for it so I gave up. What it reminded me of is that, having a long term goal and plan, is something you have to chip away at everyday. I keep a diary so I’m good at documenting things however my planning and goal-setting needed some work.

I created an excel sheet. The first sheet (Tasks) is a large list of tasks and if they are related, they are stacked next to each other. The second sheet (9 to 5) shows the schedule for the day from 9am to 1pm and 1pm to 5pm. There is a third sheet but it’s just a list of things that I may want to buy at some point in the future.

The S means that the task has been started and the F means it has been finished. Once a task is completed and I don’t need to see it anymore, I move it down with all the other completed tasks. The word passion just reminds me to do things with purpose and fun, to live life fully.

I’m quite strict about the morning (9am to 1pm) and from 2pm to 5pm I’m more flexible. Ideally, at the end of each day, I create a list of things that I WANT to do and then achieve as much of it as possible. Essentially, I’ve organized almost all of my activities (coding, writing, exercise, shopping, video watching, cleaning, etc) and for the past week or two it has been extremely helpful.



Another thing that I try to do, to make tasks more engaging, is to connect them to another more pleasurable activity. When I cook, I also listen to jazz FM hoping for a good tune to come on so I can sing along. Also I used to watch a video (TV) for 20 about minutes whilst holding an abdominal crunch. I need to do that more. Two birds with one stone. With regard to coding practice, it has become habitual because of my GitHub activity bar. It’s become like a game. Every weekday I try to do at least 4 updates (push code to server). This ensures the green square for the day is a nice shade of green. Actually these green squares are probably what inspired me sub-consciously to make the “completed” squares in the excel sheet green.
