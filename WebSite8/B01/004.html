READING BOOKS
To paraphrase Walter Mosely, “Is it better to read 50 books once or 10 books, five times each?” I have not read widely but I have read a lot, the same books, over and over again. A book is worked on for a long time and usually contains a number of layers and threads which allows for multiple readings. The same goes for my favourite movies and TV shows. I’ve seen Ripley, Joe Black, Grosse Pointe, Ed, etc, many times and often notice things that I didn’t before because my perspective has changed slightly. Also do you really need to listen to Elvis if you’ve already listened to a lot of Chuck Berry?

INFLUENCES
When asked about influences, Stephen Fry always cites three authors that had a big impact on him, the three W’s, Woodhouse, Waugh and Wilde. My style was influenced by what I enjoyed reading when I first started reading novels. Hemingway’s poetic concision, Plath’s disarming honesty and Sartre’s subversive thoughts and Bret Easton Ellis’s dark wit.
